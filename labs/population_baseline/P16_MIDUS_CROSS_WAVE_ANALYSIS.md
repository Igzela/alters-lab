# Phase 16 MIDUS Cross-Wave Analysis

**Date:** 2026-06-03
**Status:** Aggregate cross-wave comparison completed

## Summary

Phase 16 performed aggregate cross-wave analysis of MIDUS data across three waves (1995-2014). This is descriptive comparison only - not individual longitudinal linkage.

## Cross-Wave Comparison

| Variable | MIDUS 1 (1995-96) | MIDUS 2 (2004-06) | MIDUS 3 (2013-14) | Trend |
|----------|-------------------|-------------------|-------------------|-------|
| Self-Rated Health | 3.53 | 2.46 | 2.57 | Improved then stable |
| Life Satisfaction | 7.70 | 7.76 | 7.78 | Slight increase |
| Neuroticism | 2.24 | 2.07 | 2.06 | Decreased |
| Perceived Mastery | 5.84 | 5.74 | 5.60 | Slight decrease |
| Age | 46.38 | 55.43 | 63.64 | +9 years per wave |

## Key Observations

### Health Outcomes
- **Self-rated health** improved from MIDUS 1 to MIDUS 2 (3.53 → 2.46 on 1-5 scale)
- Note: Scale direction may differ across waves (check variable definitions)
- Stable between MIDUS 2 and MIDUS 3

### Subjective Wellbeing
- **Life satisfaction** shows slight increase across waves (7.70 → 7.78)
- Consistently high (above midpoint on 0-10 scale)

### Personality Traits
- **Neuroticism** decreased slightly (2.24 → 2.06)
- **Perceived mastery** shows slight decline (5.84 → 5.60)

### Demographics
- **Age** increases ~9 years per wave (longitudinal cohort)
- Sample sizes decrease due to attrition (7,108 → 4,963 → 3,294)

## Methodology Notes

### What This Analysis Does
- ✅ Aggregate cross-wave comparison
- ✅ Descriptive statistics per wave
- ✅ Mean/median comparisons

### What This Analysis Does NOT Do
- ❌ Individual longitudinal linkage
- ❌ Causal aging effects
- ❌ Controlling for cohort/attrition
- ❌ Statistical significance testing

## Limitations

1. **Attrition bias:** Sample sizes decrease across waves
2. **Cohort effects:** Not controlled - interpreting as aging effects is misleading
3. **Scale comparability:** Some variables may use different scales across waves
4. **No individual tracking:** Cannot link respondents across waves
5. **Descriptive only:** No causal claims possible

## Proper Interpretation

**Use this language:**
- "Cross-wave descriptive pattern"
- "Aggregate comparison across survey waves"
- "Sample means differ across waves"

**Do NOT use this language:**
- "Aging effects"
- "Longitudinal change"
- "Causal impact of time"
